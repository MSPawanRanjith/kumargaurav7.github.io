				The IEEE MSRIT Student Chapter & EMB MSRIT student chapter in association with iDoc med solution is organizing a special interactive session on
				MEDICAL INNOVATIONS FOR THE MASSES :OPPORTUNITIES tomorrow, 3rd March 2017.
				Venue: LHC Seminar Hall1 , RIT Campus
				  Time: 3pm to  4.30pm

				Overview:
				India specifically and south asia as a whole has a whole set of medical issues common as well as specific to the region. In view of large population it creates large opportunities to solve these issues within constraints specific to the region. Innovative methodologies and solutions are required.

				Presently 85% of medical devices are imported at huge costs to the end user (patients) and also loss to the governments in terms of revenue as well as brain drain. In real terms technology development within the country has short cycle time due to availability of excellent surgeons, design and manufacturing engineers.

				The debate today would be to develop cost effective medical products with an integration of surgeons, engineers, testing, quality standards and manufacturing setups.

				The students, faculty of engineering and medical streams jointly taking up projects and contribution towards product design life cycle with continuous interactions between them might provide a solution for the above issues.

				Consultant Surgical Gastroenterologist at M.S. RamaiahMemorial Hospital
				Dr Rao graduated (MBBS) from University College of Medical Sciences, Delhi in 1991, obtained his Masters in Surgery (MS) from Kasturba Medical College, Mangalore and then did a year of senior residency in Cancer Surgery at Safdarjung Hospital, Delhi. He later received his Fellowship in Transplantation Surgery from Australian National Liver Transplantation Unit, Royal Prince Alfred Hospital, Sydney, Australia. He shifted to Hong Kong to acquire further training in HepatoPancreato Biliary (HPB) surgery and Liver transplantation at the Prince of Wales Hospital, the Chinese University of Hong Kong. After a stint at these centre’s of excellence for over 9 years ,Dr Rao shifted to Bangalore in 2005 and practices Surgical Gastroenterology and specializes in HPB surgery (diseases including cancers of Liver, Gall bladder, Bile ducts and Pancreas) at MS Ramaiah Memorial Hospital and MS Ramaiah Medical College, Bangalore, India. Dr Rao has over 40, mainly international publications to his credit including seminal work in the area of viral transfer in Bioartificial liver. and speaks at national and international conferences. Dr. Rao also involves in Clinical trials and is presently engaged with the industry in innovating/creating surgical instruments and technologies
