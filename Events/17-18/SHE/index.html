About the event

Until a couple of decades ago, women in India used scrap cloth from old saris or towels during their menstruation. In a country where the bodily function of menstruation was and still is a taboo subject, with the accompanying shame, stigma and myths surrounding it, many women still reuse pieces of old cloth. Disposable sanitary napkins are being proposed as a solution to this. While there is no debate that women and girls need to be provided alternatives to the old methods which made them highly susceptible to infection, the solution of disposable sanitary napkins, is creating a whole slew of new problems in a country like ours with a large population and poor waste disposal systems. Luckily there are solutions which are ecologically safe, affordable and safe for a woman's body. These are the menstrual cup and the washable cloth pads and these are available in India. Women who have made the switch to cups or cloth pads are happy, because of the benefits (no rash, itching), the economics (recover costs in less than 1 year) and the convenience. Unfortunately these options are not advertised and awareness about them is very low. Our campaign called ‘Green the Red’ is a small effort to fill this gap of information. We conduct awareness sessions, talks in Corporates, Anganwdis, Government Departments, NGOs and set up outreach tables at women oriented events such as the Pinkathons, Contours Women's Day runs, BBMP Compost Santhes and Swacha Graha. We have collaborated with NGOs who work with adolescent girls to take our message to the high schools. All this has given our fledgling campaign small 'wings'. We aim to engage with the government and influence changes at policy level for promotion of sustainable menstrual hygiene options only.

      The Speakers

      We are a volunteer collective of individual eco-warriors and eco-retailers passionate about changing the menstrual hygiene landscape in India by advocating and building awareness on sustainable menstrual hygiene practices and management solutions that are safe for our bodies, safe for the environment, safe for the sanitation workers and reduce the burden on our waste disposal systems. Our aim is to improve women’s health and ultimately public health through sustainable menstrual practices.

She moved to the development field to work with NGOs with a focus on education and children.Waste Management is her personal passion and is very much linked with her work with children.       Dr. Meenakshi Bharath

      Dr Meenakshi Bharath is not only a Gynaecologist and Fertility specialist but she’s also a green campaigner, an advocate for Clean India, a strident voice for garbage segregation/ reduction and recycling, and a relentless fighter to create visibility for the problems of voiceless people. Her current project is " Green the red" campaign -advocating " Sustainable menstruation" a solution that makes the life of women very happy during this sometimes ,stressful period every month.

      Mrs. Smita Kulkarni

      Smita Kulkarni is a techie turned graphic designer turned environmental activist. Her work experience includes 8 years as a software professional in General Electric, 8 years as a Graphic Design Consultant and 5 years of teaching at Design Colleges latest at St. Joseph's. She started with herself: segregating and composting her household waste, looking for environment friendly alternatives to plastic and everything that we use in our life which could create garbage. She is part of the citizen engagement program of BBMP in her area and now an active eco-retailer of sustainable menstruation products.
